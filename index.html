Password Security Tips

Key Takeaways:

  • Never reuse passwords across different websites/accounts
  • Use long passphrases (at least 12 characters) with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Consider using a password manager like Bitwarden (free) or LastPass
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) whenever possible
PRO TIP: Create Memorable Passphrases

Instead of "password123", try something like: "MyCatLoves2EatTunaFish!" or "SummerVacation2023@TheBeach". These are long, include different character types, and are easy to remember!

Social Engineering Defense

Scenario: The Urgent Message

You receive a direct message on Instagram from a friend's account that was recently hacked. The message seems urgent and asks for help. How do you respond?

Message from @jessica_smith (your friend)
"Hey! I'm in a really bad situation. I lost my phone and I'm locked out of my account. Can you help me by receiving a verification code and sending it to me? I'll send it to your number."

What would you do?

Red Flags to Look For:

  • Urgency: Creates panic so you act without thinking
  • Asking for verification codes: These are meant for YOU only
  • Unusual requests: Friends shouldn't ask for codes/passwords
  • Emotional manipulation: Uses friendship to pressure you

2. What would happen if you sent the verification code?
You would help your friend get back into their account

Nothing - verification codes are harmless
You would receive a reward for helping
3. What should you do when you receive this message?
Send the code to help your friend quickly
Ignore it - it's probably not important
Contact your friend through another method (call, text, see them in person) to verify
Reply asking for more information

Social Engineering Defense Tips

Key Takeaways:

  • Never share verification codes with anyone - they're for your eyes only
  • Verify unusual requests through a different communication method
  • Be suspicious of urgency - scammers create panic to prevent thinking
  • Protect your friends - if an account is hacked, let them know
REAL-WORLD EXAMPLE: Instagram "Friend in Need" Scam

This is a real scam happening right now! Hackers take over accounts, then message the person's friends asking for verification codes. If you send the code, the hacker can:

  1. Take over YOUR account
  2. Access your friend's private information
  3. Message more people from both accounts
  4. Steal personal data or money
