Question 1 of 3
Why was it SO easy for Steve to hack ALL of Alex's accounts at once?
A Alex's computer had a virus
B He reused the same weak password on every single account
C The websites were all hacked at the same time
D Alex shared his password with a friend
💡Credential stuffing! When you reuse passwords, hackers try your leaked password on every website automatically. One password = a master key to your whole digital life.
Question 2 of 3
Which of these passwords would actually survive a hacker attack?
A fluffy2010 (my cat's name + birth year)
B P@ssw0rd! (classic substitution)
C Purple$Elephant!RunsFast42 (random passphrase)
D qwerty123 (keyboard pattern)
🔐Passphrases are king! Random word combinations are nearly impossible to guess yet easy to remember. Hackers crack "P@ssw0rd!" in seconds — it's on every cracking dictionary. Length beats complexity every time!
Question 3 of 3
A website emails you: "Your account may have been in a data breach." What do you do FIRST?
A Do nothing — the website will fix it
B Create a new account with the same password
C Immediately change that password AND all accounts using it, enable 2FA
D Delete the email — probably spam
🚨Act fast! Hackers sell breached credentials within minutes. Change passwords immediately, enable 2FA everywhere, and check haveibeenpwned.com to see if your email was leaked.
Password Mission Complete! Your cheat sheet:
  • Use a different password for EVERY account — a password manager remembers them all
  • Make passwords 16+ characters: mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Passphrases like "Purple$Elephant!RunsFast" are strong AND memorable
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) everywhere you can
  • Check haveibeenpwned.com — your email may already be in a breach!

Question 1 of 3
What manipulation technique is Steve using to make the DM so convincing?
A He installed a virus on your phone
B Trusted identity + artificial urgency + emotional pressure
C He hacked Instagram's servers directly
D He sent a virus link hidden in the message
🎭The social engineering trifecta! (1) Trusted identity — looks like your real friend, (2) Urgency — "RIGHT NOW!", (3) Emotional pressure — "I'm in trouble 🙏". This combo bypasses your logical thinking. No hacking needed — just human psychology!
Question 2 of 3
If you send that 6-digit code, exactly what happens to YOUR account?
A Nothing — codes are just harmless numbers
B You successfully help Layla log back in
C Steve uses the code to take over YOUR account immediately
D Instagram detects the fraud and blocks Steve automatically
🔓That code IS your account! It's Instagram's login code sent to YOUR number to log into YOUR account. Steve types it in and locks you out in under 60 seconds — then changes your email and password. Remember: no legitimate service will ever ask for an authentication code.
Question 3 of 3
You receive this urgent DM. What is the SMARTEST response?
A Send the code quickly — she's your best friend!
B Ask more questions in the DM to verify
C Close the DM and call/WhatsApp Layla directly to confirm it's really her
D Report the account to Instagram and do nothing else
📞Verify through a DIFFERENT channel! The entire scam depends on you staying in that compromised DM. One real phone call breaks the illusion instantly. Layla will say "What? I didn't send anything!" — Steve is caught red-handed.
Social Engineering Mission Complete! Key defences:
  • NEVER share verification codes, OTPs, or passwords — not even with "friends"
  • Urgency and panic are manipulation tactics — slow down, think, then verify
  • Always confirm urgent requests through a completely different channel
  • If an account sends suspicious messages, warn the real person immediately
  • Enable login notifications so you know when someone tries to access your accounts

Question 1 of 3
Why are DeepFake videos MORE dangerous than a simple photoshopped image?
A They are bigger files that crash phones
B Video + audio together are far more convincing — our brains are hardwired to trust moving faces and familiar voices
C They contain hidden malware
D They can only be made by governments
🧠Seeing AND hearing = believing! Humans evolved to trust faces and voices above all else. This is why deepfakes are used for financial scams and fake news — they exploit our hardwired biology, triggering instant trust before our critical thinking kicks in.
Question 2 of 3
You're watching a suspicious video. Which is the STRONGEST sign it could be a DeepFake?
A The person is wearing a hat
B Lips don't perfectly sync with the words; the face edges look slightly blurry
C The video is filmed vertically on a phone
D The background is a plain solid colour
👁️Key visual glitches to spot: AI struggles with (1) perfect lip sync, (2) face edges — look for blur where face meets hair/background, (3) blinking — too regular or too rare, (4) lighting — shadows that don't match the scene. Pause and look carefully!
Question 3 of 3
Your "cousin" sends you a WhatsApp video saying they're abroad in trouble and need £300 transferred urgently. Your BEST response is:
A Transfer the money immediately — it's family!
B Reply in WhatsApp asking for more proof
C Hang up and call their REAL known number, or ask a secret family code word via voice call
D Send half the money first as a test
🔐Set a family code word! Agree on a secret word only your family knows. If anyone claims to be family in an emergency, ask for the code word. Can't say it? It's a scam. Also always call back on their KNOWN saved number — not the number that called you.
DeepFake Mission Complete! Stay AI-proof:
  • Pause & check: lip-sync issues, blurry face edges, unnatural blinking
  • Verify any urgent video request through a completely different channel
  • Create a family "code word" for emergencies — scammers won't know it
  • Use InVID or FotoForensics to analyse suspicious videos online
  • Always check if the video appears on the person's official verified account
