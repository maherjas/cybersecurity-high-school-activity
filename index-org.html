Password Power-Up

The Problem: Alex's Password Habits

Alex is a high school student who uses the same simple password for everything: "alex2023". He uses it for his school account, email, social media, and gaming accounts. Last week, his favorite game was hacked, and now his other accounts are being accessed by someone else!

1. Why is using the same password for multiple accounts dangerous?
If one account is hacked, ALL your accounts can be hacked

Websites don't allow the same password for multiple accounts

2. Which of these is the MOST secure password strategy?
Using your pet's name followed by your birth year

3. What should you do if a website you use has a data breach?

Password Security Tips

Key Takeaways:

  • Never reuse passwords across different websites/accounts
  • Use long passphrases (at least 12 characters) with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Consider using a password manager like Bitwarden (free) or LastPass
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) whenever possible

Scenario: The Urgent Message

You receive a direct message on Instagram from a friend's account that was recently hacked. The message seems urgent and asks for help. How do you respond?

Social engineering / account takeover

2. What would happen if you sent the verification code?

3. What should you do when you receive this message?

Social Engineering Defense Tips

Key Takeaways:

  • Never share verification codes with anyone - they're for your eyes only
  • Verify unusual requests through a different communication method
  • Be suspicious of urgency - scammers create panic to prevent thinking
  • Protect your friends - if an account is hacked, let them know

DeepFake Detection Challenge

What are DeepFakes?

DeepFakes are AI-generated videos, images, or audio that make it look like someone is saying or doing something they never actually did. They're created using artificial intelligence and can be very convincing!

Real Example: In 2023, a fake video of a celebrity endorsing a scam cryptocurrency made people lose millions of dollars!

How to Spot the DeepFake?

Real Video
Natural blinking
Syncs with audio
Consistent lighting

DeepFakes are getting better, but they still have subtle flaws that careful observers can spot!

DeepFakes aren't just videos! AI can clone anyone's voice with just a few seconds of audio.

DeepFake Defense Tips

How to Protect Yourself:

  • Verify through multiple channels - call, text, or meet in person
  • Look for inconsistencies - strange blinking, lighting, or audio sync
  • Check the source - is it from a trusted, official account?
  • Be skeptical of urgent requests especially involving money
  • Use fact-checking websites for viral videos
FUN FACT: DeepFakes in Pop Culture

DeepFakes aren't all bad! They're used in movies to de-age actors (like in "The Irishman") and to bring historical figures to life in documentaries. The key is using this technology ethically and being aware of its potential for harm.
